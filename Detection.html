Introduction

Cyclic voltammetry is a method of changing the potential to obtain the direction of the redox current. Mainly by applying a cyclic potential, from a starting potential to a terminal potential at a fixed rate, and then changing back to the starting potential at the same rate, thereby drawing a CV of a reversible oxidation reactant analysis for the cycle. By measuring the value of the original electrode material and the change in the value of the connected DNA material, it can be determined that the electrode is connected to the probe.
