Abstract

In recent years, the problem of drug resistance genes caused by antibiotic abuse has become more and more serious, and so cause the superstrong drug-resistance bacteria exist. In order to solve this situation, we designed a biosensor to quickly identify drug resistance genes, and named this project "briDge aN erA : FF".

Biochip "FF" features two specific conductive electrodes on it. In addition, we emerged two characteristics of DNA on this biochip: complementary and electric conduction. By using the complementary characteristics of DNA, two different capture probes are designed from drug resistance genes, then respectively connected onto two electrodes. The characteristics of DNA conduction are used as connected bridges, through simply measure the electric signal changes, we can not only detect longer segment DNA sequences even more quickly, but also detect multiple gene in parallel to increase the accuracy of drug administration and slow down the rate of drug resistance derive.
